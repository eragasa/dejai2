- Wilsdorf, Janis, et al. “Validation without Data: Formalizing Stylized Facts of Time Series.” _Proceedings of the 2024 Winter Simulation Conference_, IEEE, 2024.
    
    - Reason: Treats stylized facts of time series (e.g., volatility clustering) as empirical regularities that precede and constrain mechanistic models, a direct modern analog of pre‑law “facts” in physical and simulated systems.[philipp-andelfinger+1](https://philipp-andelfinger.net/pdfs/wilsdorf2023validation.pdf)​
        
- Shimokawa, Tetsuya, et al. “An Agent‑Based Approach to Financial Stylized Facts.” _Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and Its Applications_, vol. 379, no. 1, 2007, pp. 207–225.
    
    - Reason: Uses agent‑based models to reproduce well‑known financial stylized facts (fat tails, volatility clustering), showing how natural‑science‑style modeling works backward from regularities to mechanisms.[ejpam+1](https://www.ejpam.com/index.php/ejpam/article/view/801/122)​
